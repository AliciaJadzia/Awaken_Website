Welcome to Awaken
A caring and smart alarm clock for neurodivergent people.

Objective:
Build a smart alarm clock that can wake up the heaviest sleeper, track metrics, and provide insight on sleeping patterns.
Why:
As someone with ADHD, I struggle with insomnia, inconsistent sleep patterns, and executive dysfunction, as well as the “Can’t sleep because going to sleep requires being bored for a small amount of time” phenomenon. Although medication helps, sticking to a sleep routine is still hard.
Metrics to track:
  • T2W: Time to wake up, time between alarm and cancellation of alarm.
  • ESAT: Effective sleep attempt time, last time when the phone was active due to user input.
  • TUS: Time of undisturbed sleep, time between ESAT and Alarm.
  • SRC: Sleep reincidence counter, periodic check of awakeness. (Initial interval=10 minutes, Interval doubles each time, 4 iterations, {10, 20, 40, 80}, 3H 10 M total.)
Tech stack:
  • Mobile app: Flutter
  • Database: Cloud Firestore
  • Authentication: Firebase Auth
  • Backend: Cloud Functions
  • Website: Firebase Hosting
  • User updates: Firebase Cloud Messaging
Reporting:
A Cloud function is triggered at an arbitrary time of the day, it calculates the last sleep cycle’s metrics and uses Firebase Cloud Messaging to push a notification or message to the user with this information.
A Dashboard website is also available through Firebase Hosting with real time updates on metrics, and a more detailed view of sleep statistics.
This would include, monthly, weekly, and all time.
Report statistics:
T2W, ESAT, TUS Average, Median, Maximum, Minimum
SRC Best days, (no reincidence), Worst days, (high reincidence)
SDW: Sleep Deprivation Warning Triggered by: TUS under 6 hours AND SRC >= 3
Burnout Warning Triggered by: SDW Three days of the same week OR Two days in a row.
ERO: Emergency Reach Out Triggered by: Two Burnout Warnings in same week.
Motivational logic:
As with reporting, when a good or best day is had, a notification or message will be sent to give the user some positive feedback and motivate them to keep on schedule.
Medication, Hydration, and Food Reminder:
Notifications throughout the day reminding the user to drink water, take any needed meds, and check if they are hungry. People with ADHD experience time blindness and hyperfocus, which can lead to spending large periods of time without checking on our own bodily needs, this can leave us unaware that we are hungry, or thirsty. These reminders must be non imperative in their phrasing, and instead be framed as questions. This encourages being mindful without self blame, guilt, or triggering trauma response. Some examples would include: Are you thirsty? Do you feel hungry? How about a drink of water? All of these questions are non confrontational.
Early warning and auto reach out system:
SDW and Burnout Warning notifications, worded with care and messages of reassurance to prevent self blame and spirals. ERO sends a message to a designated contact who is a trusted friend or relative that has agreed to check in and provide support.
